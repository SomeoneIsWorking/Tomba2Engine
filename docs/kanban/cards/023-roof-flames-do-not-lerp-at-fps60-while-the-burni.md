**2026-07-23:** 2026-07-23 (investigated during #33, NOT fixed — scoped). Mechanism CONFIRMED structurally: game/render/fx_sprite.cpp fxSpriteTap emits its sprite quads via emitOrQueue with xsf/ysf = nullptr, i.e. has_xyf==0. isTier1Owned (fps60.cpp) is false for has_xyf==0 RQ_WORLD, so these prims present VERBATIM from mRqCur on BOTH frame kinds -> step at 30Hz -> no lerp. The rope flame lerps because it comes from a display-pass producer (QuadRtptSubmit) that tier1Render re-runs under the lerped camera. GRADUATION PATH (the REDIRECT doctrine; NOT a matcher/stamp): fxSpriteTap fires at GUEST time (leaf tap on FUN_80027A4C, per-object beh_cull_tick_render), so it cannot simply 're-run at present'. It must (1) CAPTURE per emitter-instance at guest time the RAW world anchor (node+0x2C/+0x30), the record-list bytes, clutPage, and the caller-specific IR0 scale basis (FUN_80027CB4/E5C/281EC each modulate scale differently -> needs their formula RE'd); (2) at present, re-RTPS the world anchor through the LERPED camera (mCamOverride) -> new anchorX/Y + SZ3, re-derive scale, re-derive the quads host-side. This is the actor-transform-tier lerp (lerp the projected anchor, then draw the same way), one frame behind, no guest re-run, no packet fingerprinting. It is a real multi-step port, deferred to its own session. #33 (done) removed the perf pressure that was making fps60 work expensive to iterate on.
