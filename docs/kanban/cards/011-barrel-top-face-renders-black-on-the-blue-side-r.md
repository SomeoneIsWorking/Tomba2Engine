**2026-07-22:** USER 2026-07-22 with two screenshots. The water-pump barrel: on the BLUE side its top/rim face renders solid BLACK (wrong); on the RED side the same face renders correctly (red). Both shots are the same object from different angles, so this is face/normal or CLUT dependent rather than a missing texture - a black face next to a correct one usually means the primitive is drawn with no texture bound (or a zero CLUT), or is back-face-shaded. Leads: Panel::fillQuad is NOT it (2D UI). Start from the per-object emitters just ported - Render::subPartWalk (LIVE, 139 hits) submits per-sub-part geomblks, and the barrel is a multi-part node. Compare the two angles under SBS (pane A pc_faithful vs pane B oracle) to see whether psx_render shows the same black face; if the oracle is correct and ours is black, it is ours.

**2026-07-22:** 2026-07-22 ROOT-CAUSED (not yet fixed). Headless repro found: same exec (PSXPORT_GATE=1) with pc_render vs PSXPORT_ORACLE=1 psx_render, seesaw-weight.pad + walk left/up - pc_render draws the barrel top BLACK, psx_render draws the blue water (scratch/screenshots/cmp/barrelcmp.png). PRIMAT at (165,92): exactly two world prims, the SAME four screen vertices - a dark cap (seq=726, tp 704,0) and the water surface (seq=746, tp 640,0). The guest paints cap-then-water; PSX has no depth buffer so the water wins. We give both real per-vertex depth and the buffer picks the cap, because the cap is genuinely ~3e-5 nearer AND the non-planar quad is triangulated along different diagonals in the two submissions (gap inflated to 5.8e-4, sign flips with angle = black one side, correct the other). PROVED the resolver: PSXPORT_ZBIAS=3e-5 PSXPORT_ZBIAS_MAX=0.5 renders the water, matching the oracle (proof.png) - and PROVED the global knob cannot ship, it wrecks the rest of the frame (full3.png). Fix direction: one shared depth per object so the pair is an exact tie; blocker is dbg_node=0 on OT-walk-teed prims. Full writeup in docs/findings/render.md.

**2026-07-22:** 2026-07-22 (2nd session) Attempted the fix, got a CORRECT picture, reverted it anyway. Two corrections to the earlier analysis, both measured: (1) the two prims are NOT duplicate quads - the integer xy in primat-rq is rounded, the float corners differ and they are in different geomblks (801C8F58 vs 801CA55C); they are a near-edge-on interior wall and the water plane whose depth fields genuinely cross. (2) The 5.8e-4 gap is therefore NOT a triangulation artifact - a least-squares plane fit per quad moved it only 12 percent. What DID work: a within-object paint-order rank (small camera-ward step per submission) renders the water correctly and matches the oracle with the rest of the frame intact (fixcmp3.png). Reverted because the grouping is not real: cur_render_node falls back to the guest scratchpad 0x1F80028C at the GT3/GT4 submit site, and the whole per-object pass collapses onto ONE node (736 quads, screen-wide bbox, 6+ geomblks) - so it is the frame-global ramp already ruled out, with step and cap tuned until this scene looked right. NEXT (specific): open diag.beginObject(node) around the per-object mesh walk feeding Render::gt3/gt4 in game/render/submit.cpp - the same scope perobj_dispatch.cpp:261, perobj_billboard.cpp:757 and native_terrain.cpp:142 already open. That is also the root of dbg_node==0 on these prims. With real per-object runs the step can be ~4e-6 and needs no tuning. Grouping by currentGeomblk does NOT work (measured).

**2026-07-22:** 2026-07-22 USER: 'isn't this resolvable by more porting?' - YES, and the measurement confirms it. debug otattr names the frame's RTPS producers; the two that dominate are 0x8003F9A8 (787+298+31 calls) and 0x80146478 (~850), and codemap says NO native owner for both. The barrel's faces are emitted by SUBSTRATE code, so the engine never sees the object - only anonymous quads arriving at the OT with GTE depth. Every renderer-side fix I tried was re-deriving, inside the rasterizer, an identity and draw order the emitter already has. Breaks the project's own rule: don't debug a divergence before the call chain is owned end-to-end. Also tried and reverted: opening diag.beginObject in the already-owned subPartWalk/sharedTransformWalk - the barrel's quads never pass through them (0x8003F698 below them IS owned = Render::perModeDispatch, so the chain looked complete from that side; the geometry comes from the unowned emitters above). NEXT: port 0x8003F9A8 and 0x80146478. Card blocked on that, not on a render rule.
